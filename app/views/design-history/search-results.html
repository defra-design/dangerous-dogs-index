First iteration of the search results

For the initial iteration of the search results, we decided which data to display through consultation with a key stakeholder. However, as we didn’t have access to the DDI team at that time, we were unable to conduct research into their specific needs.

One starting assumption was that, when searching, the team would be presented with a large number of search results and would need to scan them quickly to identify the correct record.

For this reason we developed a horizontal layout for the search results, aligning with common patterns used in other online search engines.

First usability tests

The search results performed well in testing. We identified one simple improvement to assist some users – underlining the dog ED number to clearly indicate it as a link.

Alpha assessment feedback

The alpha assessment design assessor provided recommendations for improving the search results:

  • review the use of the summary card component on the search results page
  • check for sufficient colour contrast between the text and the background of the status labels

While refining the search results design, we also considered whether displaying the dog owner’s address would help users identify the correct result.

We tested the updated design with users during private beta.

Outcome of usability testing

The new design was well received by users, as it eliminated the need to click into a dog or owner record to view the address. This allowed them to find the correct record more quickly.
