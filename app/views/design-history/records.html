DDI team needs

The initial versions of the dog and owner record pages were developed without prior research into the needs of the DDI team.

Our starting assumptions were:

  • the standard ‘one thing per page’ approach wouldn’t suit a case management system where the same users edit records daily
  • users would need to see all information related to a record on a single screen without excessive scrolling

These assumptions led us to adopt a two-column design using the summary list component.

To quickly deliver a usable first version of the Index, we designed the initial layout to include edit functionality for all three details sections on single screens, again not using a ‘one thing per page’ approach. We also chose not to include a ‘success’ screen.

When we later tested this approach for usability, we found no issues with editing the information.

Check history

The DDI team needed a way to check the activity on a record. To address this, we added a button to the dog and owner records linking to a page that displayed a chronological history of record activity.

The first iteration of this page was named ‘Check activity’. Following our Alpha assessment, the design assessor recommended renaming it to ‘Review history’.

We adopted the recommendation but opted for ‘Check history’ as it better reflected the task the team would perform.

Meeting police needs

The team’s starting assumption was that a read-only version of the Index would meet the police user needs. For the first iteration of the dog and owner records for the police, we simply removed all the editing functionality.

Download the record history

Like the DDI team, the police needed to check the history of a record.

Research during Alpha uncovered an additional need, the ability to download a copy of the records along with their history. To meet this need, we added the functionality to the ‘Check history’ page.

We surfaced the new functionality on the dog and owner record pages, relabelling the ‘Check history’ button to ‘Check and download history’.

Tracking CDO progress

Another research finding revealed that police needed a clear view of a CDO’s progress to help them effectively manage their kennel space. While processes vary by force, some DLOs assist dog owners in submitting the necessary information to Defra.

Speeding up the CDO processing time helps reduce kennel time for dogs and frees up valuable space.

We added a link on the dog record for the police to ‘Check CDO progress’.

The ‘CDO progress’ page shows the police which documents Defra has received from the dog owner and the date of receipt.

We added a simple form linked from the CDO progress page, allowing police to submit microchipping and neutering details online instead of using the paper ‘Form 2’.
